PATH FORWARD FOR DOCUMENTARIES

Looking ahead, what are the interesting developments in SEA documentaries? In Path Forward for Documentaries, the panelists will share their experiences with conception, financing, production, and distribution of their work and how the rise of online platforms are affecting their approach to their work.

Puangsoi Aksornsawang

— a filmmaker from Thailand

Kavich Neang

— a filmmaker from Cambodia

Jewel Maranan

FROM SHORTS TO FIRST FEATURES

Many students and young filmmakers struggle to make the leap from shorts to their first feature film. In From Shorts to First Features, the panelists will share their experiences as producers and directors and how they transitioned from the university campus to becoming working professionals in the SEA film industry.

Lai Weijie

— a producer from Singapore

Davy Chou

— a producer and director from Cambodia

Monster Jimenez

— a producer and director from Philippines

FEMALE CINEMATOGRAPHERS

Often, films tend to take a long time and cost a lot of money to make. The 48-Hour Film Challenge will force you to break these “rules” and work quickly and instinctually, the same as painting a picture or playing a guitar. Participants will meet on Thursday, July 4, 2019 for a talk by mentors Nottapon Boonprakob and xxxxxxxx, and receive the prompt. They will then have 48 hours to write, shoot, and edit their three-minute film to turn in by Saturday, July 6, 2019. Finally, all the films will be screened together for the public on Sunday, July 7, 2019. Prizes will be awarded by a jury. This workshop is limited to 35 participants (can be teams).

Nottapon Boonprakob

— a filmmaker from Thailand

Graiwoot Chulapongsathorn

— a film critic and professor from Thailand
